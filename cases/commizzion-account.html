Rebuilding a desktop-only platform into a mobile-first product ecosystem

Company Inlaze
Year 2024–2025
Type Platform · Design System
Role Product Designer
Overview

Commizzion Account had outgrown its original architecture. After years of consistent growth, the platform needed a structural evolution to stay competitive — built exclusively for desktop, with high information density and a flat visual hierarchy. The redesign rebuilt the product from the ground up: a unified design system, a mobile-first cross-platform experience, and a research-driven iteration process.

Process
01

Unified CM Design System

Before any screen was designed, the ecosystem needed one source of truth. The CM Design System unified tokens, typography, and components across Account, Hub, the marketing website, and Backoffice — eliminating interface fragmentation and accelerating development velocity across every product.

02

Mobile-first transition

Account.commizzion.com was built exclusively for desktop. The interface prioritized system logic over user logic — information density was high, hierarchy was flat, and there was no responsive consideration at all. The redesign turned one touchpoint into three: a mobile-first product experience, a centralized training hub, and a conversion-optimized marketing site — each built to work seamlessly across every device.

03

Data-driven iteration

We moved beyond assumptions by implementing a dual-research strategy. Usability testing, Microsoft Clarity behavioral analysis, and direct affiliate interviews uncovered the qualitative "why" behind affiliate behavior — informing several rounds of iteration on core features like campaign management.

Results

Key learnings

"A platform's growth ceiling isn't traffic — it's whether the interface can scale across new devices, contexts, and behaviors."

"Assumptions don't scale. Validating decisions with real usage data turns iteration from guesswork into strategy."
